# Incident Investigation Report: Floating Roof Seal Fire at TK-15

**Document ID:** INC-2024-008-REP
**Title:** Root Cause Analysis of floating roof seal fire at Tank TK-15
**Incident Date:** August 30, 2024
**Area:** Tank Farm (Offsites)
**Plant:** IOCL Gujarat Refinery
**Lead Investigator:** R. Sharma (Process Safety Lead)
**Operations Technician:** D. Singh

---

## 1. Description of Event
On August 30, 2024, at 14:15 hrs, a minor fire occurred at storage tank **TK-15** during high-flow product transfer. The fire was located at the primary floating roof seal area. The emergency deluge system (FF-System-2) was activated and the fire was fully extinguished by process team within 45 minutes. No injuries occurred.

---

## 2. Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
Investigation revealed the following contributing factors:
1. **Flow Velocity:** Product transfer rate at inlet was 1.5 m/s, exceeding the safety standard limit of 1.2 m/s.
2. **Static Accumulation:** High flow rate generated electrostatic charge at the crude oil inlet nozzle.
3. **Earthing Integrity:** Grounding cable connection showed high resistance due to corrosion.
4. **Vapor Concentration:** Floating roof primary seal showed minor gaps, allowing light hydrocarbons to escape.

The combination of static discharge and flammable mixture led to ignition at the seal.

---

## 3. Regulatory Compliance Gaps
The incident points to a layout and operating gap under **OISD-STD-118** and **OISD-STD-116** (Fire Protection Facilities for Petroleum Refineries). Grounding connection checks must be conducted monthly and documented.

---

## 4. Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
1. **Action 1:** Install automatic flow rate limiter at inlet to restrict flow to less than 1.2 m/s (Owner: D. Singh, Completed: Sept 15, 2024).
2. **Action 2:** Replace the earthing connection and initiate monthly grounding testing.
3. **Action 3:** Repair primary roof seals during next shutdown.
