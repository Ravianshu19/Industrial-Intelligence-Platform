## 1. Scope & Regulatory Framework
This document provides specific layout, safety distances, and condition monitoring requirements for Crude Distillation Units (CDU-1) at the IOCL Gujarat Refinery. All operating equipment must comply with the guidelines under **OISD-STD-118** (Layout and Safety) and **IS 2825** (Code for Unfired Pressure Vessels).

---

## 2. Equipment Specifications & Operational Limits

### 2.1 Heat Exchanger E-101 (CDU-1 Crude Pre-heat)
- **Equipment Tag:** E-101
- **Service:** Crude / Vacuum Residue Exchange
- **Operating Temperature:** 350°C (Tube side inlet)
- **Design Pressure:** 2.5 kg/cm²
- **Standard Reference:** API 660 (Shell-and-tube heat exchangers)
- **Vessel Code:** ASME Section VIII Div 1
- **Critical Limit:** The minimum tube wall thickness must not drop below 2.0mm. Nominal thickness is 2.77mm. Corrosion allowance is 1.0mm.
- **Maintenance History Note:** Tube bundle hydrojet cleaning was completed on Jan 8, 2025 (WO-2025-0142) by D. Singh (Technician). Under-deposit corrosion was noted at the shell inlet.

### 2.2 Distillation Column T-101
- **Equipment Tag:** T-101
- **Service:** Atmospheric Fractionator
- **Vessel Height:** 45 meters
- **Key Parameters:** Operating top temperature is 120°C. Reflux ratio is maintained at 1.8.
- **Statutory Audit Checklist:** Pressure relief valve (PSV-101) must be calibrated annually per OISD-STD-118 Section 5.2 guidelines.

### 2.3 Gas Turbine GT-101
- **Equipment Tag:** GT-101
- **Service:** Power Generation & Co-generation Unit
- **Operating Speed:** 3,000 RPM
- **Condition Monitoring Parameter:** ISO 10816-3 vibration limits. Alert threshold is set at 4.5 mm/s (1X running speed). Current trending shows +0.17 mm/s per month vibration growth.
- **Critical Action:** Bearing swap scheduled for May 2025 turnaround.

## 3. Operations & Safety Procedures

### 3.1 Emergency Shutdown (ESD) Protocol
In the event of a thermal excursion (temperature exceeding 375°C at E-101 inlet) or high LEL gas leaks, operators must trigger the safety instrumented system (SIS):
1. Activate the Emergency Shutdown Valve (ESDV-101) to isolate feed.
2. Divert process stream to the Flare System (Flare-1) via knockout drum (KO-Drum-1) to reduce pressure.
3. Check and verify that the water deluge system (FF-System-2) is active if a fire is detected (similar to the floating roof seal fire event INC-2024-008 at Tank TK-15).
